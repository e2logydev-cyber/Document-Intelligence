😰 The Old Manual Process

"Everyone Panic and Start Writing"

  • RFP arrives via email — Someone has to notice it, download it, and figure out who should handle it
  • 6 people print and read separately — Each person highlights different things, no shared understanding
  • Emergency meeting called — 90 minutes to decide who writes what. 6 people × 90 min = 9 hours burned
  • Everyone hunts through old files — "Didn't we write something like this for Acme Corp?" Searching shared drives, emails, personal folders
  • Writing in isolation — 6 people write 6 different voices. Technical lead uses jargon. Sales uses buzzwords. No consistency
  • Email ping-pong for reviews — v1, v2_Final, v2_Final_FINAL, v2_Final_FINAL_v2. Version chaos.
  • Submit and hope — Quality depends on who had time and sleep. Inconsistent, stressful, expensive.
⏱️ 3-5 days · 6 people · ₹26,000 per proposal
⚡ The New AI-Powered Process

"Upload, Review, Submit"

  • Upload RFP to one place — Drag, drop, done. 30 seconds. Everyone knows where it is.
  • AI reads and understands instantly — No human reads first. AI extracts requirements, deadlines, evaluation criteria automatically.
  • AI finds your best past work — Searches 10 years of proposals in 3 seconds. "Here are 5 similar projects we won."
  • AI assembles the dream team — "Sarah led 3 similar projects. Mike's available. This combination wins 80% of the time."
  • AI writes the first complete draft — All 5 sections written in your company's voice, with consistent terminology, in 10 minutes.
  • Experts review, don't write — Tech lead checks technical approach. Commercial checks pricing. 1 round of light edits.
  • Submit with confidence — Same quality every time. Fully cited. Fully compliant. Zero stress.
⚡ 2-4 hours · 2 people · ₹4,000 per proposal

The Complete Step-by-Step Journey

Every single action that happens from "RFP received" to "Proposal submitted" — explained in plain English.

1
📤 RFP Arrives & Gets Uploaded
Someone on your team receives an RFP email. Instead of forwarding to 6 people, they open the Proposal Intelligence web app, drag the PDF into the upload zone, and click "Start." The file goes straight to secure cloud storage. A unique tracking ID is created.
What Just Happened (Technical)
Blazor web app → Azure Blob Storage upload (encrypted) → MS SQL creates proposal record with status "Received" → SignalR notifies all connected users that new RFP arrived
2
🤖 AI Project Manager Wakes Up
The Microsoft Foundry Agent (think of it as an AI project manager) gets notified. It creates a "durable session" — basically a notebook that remembers everything about this proposal forever. It reads the blob URL and says "Got it, I'll handle this."
What Just Happened (Technical)
Foundry Agent Service creates persistent thread → Stores blob URL + proposal ID in memory → Initializes MCP tool connections (Blob, SQL, AI Search, OpenAI, Email)
3
📄 AI Reads & Understands the RFP
No human reads the RFP first. The AI uses "Document Intelligence" — a service that reads PDFs like a person, but faster. It extracts: Who's the client? What's the project? What's the budget? When's it due? What are the technical requirements? What are they evaluating us on?
What Just Happened (Technical)
Foundry calls Document Intelligence MCP tool → Async processing (AI reads 42 pages in 15 seconds) → Returns structured JSON: {client, scope, requirements[], budget, deadline, evaluationCriteria, complianceItems[]}
4
🔍 AI Searches Your Company's Brain
The AI now searches through your entire company's history — every past proposal, every team member's skills, every pricing template. But it's not just keyword search. It uses "vector search" — finding things by meaning. "CRM for manufacturing" finds projects about "customer systems for factories" even if those exact words weren't used.
What Just Happened (Technical)
Generate embedding vector from RFP → Parallel queries: MS SQL vector similarity search (cosine distance) on PastProjects + TeamProfiles + Pricing tables + Azure AI Search semantic search on unstructured documents → Hybrid ranking combines results
5
🎯 AI Assembles the Perfect Context
The AI doesn't just dump search results. It thinks: "These 3 past projects are most relevant. This team combination has won similar work. These pricing templates fit the budget range. This reference section addresses their compliance concern." It builds a "briefing packet" for the writer AI.
What Just Happened (Technical)
Foundry IQ applies iterative reflection → Evaluates retrieval confidence → Re-queries if gaps detected → Ranks by relevance + recency + win rate → Assembles context package with provenance tracking (every fact linked to source)
6
✍️ AI Writes the Complete First Draft
GPT-4o (the same AI behind ChatGPT, but enterprise-grade) receives the briefing packet and writes all 5 sections of your proposal. It writes in your company's voice because it has learned from your past winning proposals. Every claim is backed by citations to real past work.
What Just Happened (Technical)
Foundry Model Catalogue invokes GPT-4o with structured output schema → System prompt: "You are a senior proposal writer at [Company]. Write in this voice: [samples]. Cite sources." → Returns JSON matching exact schema: {executiveSummary, technicalApproach, teamProfiles, pricingNarrative, projectTimeline, citations[]}
7
💾 Draft Saved & Team Notified Instantly
The moment the AI finishes writing, the proposal appears in the web app — no refresh needed. The tech lead gets a notification: "Technical section ready for your review." The commercial lead sees pricing. Everyone sees only what they need to see.
What Just Happened (Technical)
Foundry Agent writes to MS SQL via EF Core MCP tool → SQL Query Notification triggers → SignalR pushes to Blazor clients → Role-based filtering shows relevant sections to each reviewer
8
👥 Experts Review (Don't Write, Just Validate)
Your experts log into the web app. They see a clean, professional proposal. They make comments like "Add more detail on API rate limiting" — not "Write the whole technical approach from scratch." The AI can even respond to follow-up questions: "Why did you recommend Sarah as lead?" → "She led 3 similar CRM projects, all delivered on time."
What Just Happened (Technical)
Reviewers interact with Blazor app → Comments stored in MS SQL → If changes requested, Foundry Agent re-invokes with revision instructions → Can also chat with agent via thread memory: "Why this team?" → Agent queries its memory and responds with citations
9
📤 Final Export, Submit & Learn
Once approved, the system generates a polished PDF and Word document — properly formatted, with your branding, page numbers, table of contents. It emails the final package to your bid manager. The entire process — every decision, every search, every edit — is recorded for audit and learning.
What Just Happened (Technical)
Foundry Agent calls: (1) OpenXML SDK generates PDF/DOCX via MCP, (2) Blob MCP uploads to final location with SAS token, (3) SQL MCP updates status to "Submitted", (4) Email MCP sends notification with download link, (5) Full trace written to Foundry Control Plane for audit
